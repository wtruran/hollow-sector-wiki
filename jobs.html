Jobs

Freight and defense contracts — how they work, what they pay, and how to pick the right ones.

The Job Board

The job board holds up to 20 contracts at a time — roughly split between freight and defense. It refreshes every 4 hours with a completely new pool. Contracts not accepted before the refresh are lost.

You can sort the board by Distance, Credits, SU, or Risk using the sort bar. Click a sort option again to reverse direction.

Freight Contracts

Freight jobs ask you to transport one or more cargo containers from a pickup station to a dropoff station. Each container is a fixed SU size — containers cannot be split across multiple ships, but they can ride on different ships in the same run.

Total payout = sum of container rates × distance in WU × payout scale. Upkeep is deducted from payout — the game guarantees a minimum profit of ƒ1,500 per job so short deadhead runs never run at a loss.

Risk on Freight

Freight risk is determined at job generation from the total cargo volume. Larger loads attract more pirate attention. The risk level is visible on every job card before you accept.

Defense Contracts

Defense jobs hire your fighters to escort a client convoy from one station to another. You assign fighter ships — not freighters. The convoy's risk level is set at generation and shown on the board.

For defense contracts, your assigned fighters count as both the escort and the primary ships — their combined risk aversion determines the fail chance.

Job Ratings

Every contract has a rating based on its credits-per-WU value. Ratings unlock with faction reputation — higher-rated jobs only appear when you've earned enough rep.

Job Phases

Every job moves through two phases visible on the progress bar:

  • Travel to Pickup — ships travel from their current location to the pickup station. The tick mark on the progress bar shows when this ends.
  • In Transit — convoy departs for the dropoff. This is when the attack roll happens (1–3 minutes after departure).
Ships depart from their current location, not your home base. A ship docked at a distant station will have a longer travel-to-pickup phase.

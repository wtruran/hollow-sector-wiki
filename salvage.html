Salvage

Drifting cargo is everywhere in a dead sector — if you have the eyes to spot it and a hold to recover it.

Salvage is free-floating cargo waiting to be recovered. It comes from two sources: the Salvage Scanner upgrade, which strands wreck-cargo near your stations, and piracy, which scatters whatever loot your freighter couldn't haul at a raid's kill site.

The Salvage Scanner

The Salvage Scanner is a cheap station upgrade — ƒ5,000 — that pays for itself quickly. Once installed at a station, it periodically strands a modest cargo drop within 500 WU of that station.

  • Spawn rate: ~75% chance per real hour to strand a new drop.
  • Accrues offline: rolls are replayed when you reopen the app, so drops pile up while you're away (up to the cap).
  • Cap: up to 3 uncollected drops afloat at once — recover them before they fill up and rolls are wasted.
  • Contents: a mix of raw ore and commodities, usually worth a modest 1–3k, rarely more.
Drops are anchored to the orbit of the scanner station's planet — they drift with it rather than sitting at a fixed point, so a drop's exact position moves over time.

Recovering a drop

Tap a salvage marker on the map and send a docked freighter (only freighters can haul it). The panel shows the cargo, its value, the maintenance for the round trip, and how long before the drop disperses.

  • Lifespan: a drop disperses 3 hours after it appears. Reach it before then or it's gone.
  • Range: the freighter must be able to reach the drop's intercept point within its range.
  • Delivery: recovered cargo is hauled to your nearest owned station and added to its storage.
A salvage run shows a solid trail behind the freighter (the path it has travelled) and a dashed line ahead to the drop — the same way freight routes are drawn.

Raid scatter

When you win a raid and your freighter's hold fills up, the overflow doesn't vanish — it's cut loose as a salvage drop right at the kill site, collectable within the same 3-hour window. Bring a second freighter, or come back for it before it disperses.

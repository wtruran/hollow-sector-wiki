Mining

Sending ships to asteroids, selecting materials, and surviving the return trip.

Overview

Mining is your primary source of raw materials. Open the Map, tap an asteroid, and dispatch one or more mining ships. Ships travel to the asteroid, extract material, then return to your nearest owned ship yard — where the cargo is automatically added to storage for selling or refining.

Dispatching a Mining Run

  1. Open the Map tab and tap an asteroid.
  2. In the asteroid popup, check the Risk level and available materials.
  3. Select which materials to mine (checkboxes).
  4. Assign one or more Mining ships.
  5. Optionally assign Fighter escorts to reduce attack risk on the return trip.
  6. Review the maintenance estimate, then confirm.

Asteroid Risk

Each asteroid has a Risk value displayed in its popup. This is determined by the highest-risk material available on that asteroid and re-rolled every 4 hours.

Risk ranges by material tier:

TierMaterialsRisk Range
CommonIron, Nickel, Cobalt0 – 2
UncommonIce2 – 5
RarePlatinum, Iridium, Rhodium6 – 10
The asteroid's displayed risk is the exact value used for the attack roll on the return trip. If the asteroid shows Risk 0, you cannot be attacked — even without escorts.

Travel to AsteroidShip travels from its current location to the asteroid.

ReturningShip travels back to the nearest owned ship yard. This is when the attack roll happens.

Mining Speed

Each mining ship has a mining speed multiplier. This multiplier is applied to the base mine time per CU (1.8 real seconds for all materials). Lower multiplier = faster extraction.

Total mine time = cargo capacity (CU) × base mine time (1.8 real sec/CU) × ship mining speed multiplier

Multiple Miners

You can assign multiple mining ships to the same asteroid in a single run. They all travel together and mine simultaneously — the run completes when the last ship finishes. Multiple ships also share any assigned escorts, keeping convoy costs reasonable.

Drone Mining

If your station has a Drone Bay upgrade, drones can mine autonomously without committing any player ships. Drones operate within 500 WU of their station, carry 1 SU per trip, and ignore piracy risk entirely. They loop continuously until stopped. See Station Upgrades for setup details.

Active drones appear on the map as a purple circle traveling between station and asteroid, with a faint dashed purple route line. Tap one to jump straight to its Drone Bay.

Bay buffer. Drones gather into a limited bay buffer rather than straight into storage. When the buffer fills, they pause — so an unattended bay won't pile up cargo forever. The Drone Bay screen shows the buffer level with a Move to Storage button that empties it (into your base's storage) and resumes mining.

Escort Recommendation

For common-tier asteroids with Risk 0–2, escorts are optional. For Ice (Risk 2–5) or rare materials (Risk 6–10), assign fighters whose combined aversion meets or exceeds the asteroid's current risk level to guarantee a safe return.

Enemy danger penalty applies to mining too. If your return route passes within the danger radius of an active enemy ship, the fail chance doubles. Keep an eye on the map before dispatching to high-risk sites.

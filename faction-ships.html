Faction Ships

Reward vessels that break the naming convention — earned through reputation, not bought off the rack.

At 600 reputation with a faction, that faction's signature ship unlocks for purchase in the Vendor's Faction Rewards section. These are one-of-a-kind vessels with their own names and roles, distinct from the standard SF/FX/MN fleet.

The Custodian — Iron Warden

Unlocked at 600 reputation with the Iron Warden, the Custodian is a large frigate that functions as a wandering mobile base. It never docks at a station — once commissioned, it lives on the map as a base of its own.

A base that moves

The Custodian spawns next to your home station when purchased. Tap it on the map and choose Move to relocate it anywhere — the map prompts you to tap a destination, shows the cost, and the base then travels there over real time.

  • Move cost is its maintenance for the distance, paid from credits on hand — a lien can never cover a relocation.
  • Acts as a base for every operation that returns cargo — mining, salvage, raids — counting as the nearest base wherever it's parked.
  • Goes dark in transit: while moving (or raiding) it stops acting as a base, then resumes once it settles.

The barge

Up to 10 of your ships can be stationed aboard the Custodian's barge. Send ships there with Relocate (the Custodian appears as a destination). Its 50-SU hold can't be sold from directly — load its cargo onto a barge freighter, then relocate that freighter to a real station to sell. See Logistics.

Into battle

The Custodian can lead a raid. Assign it as the flagship and its entire barge rides along, sharing the risk.

  • Combined strength: its risk aversion in a fight is its own 10 plus the aversion of everything aboard.
  • All or nothing: if the Custodian is destroyed, every ship and all cargo aboard go down with it.
  • No return: a Custodian that wins a raid holds position at the kill site — relocate it from there whenever you're ready.
The Custodian is managed entirely from the Map — move it, raid with it, and manage its hold by tapping it. Its Fleet card simply points you there.

The Synodos — The Aurel Conclave

Unlocked at 600 reputation with The Aurel Conclave, the Synodos is a roving auto-miner — a mobile Drone Bay that wanders the sector. It never docks; it lives on the map, mining nearby asteroids into its own hold while you direct it.

How it mines

Tap the Synodos on the map to open its panel, which has three tabs:

  • Drones — buy up to 20 drones (ƒ5,000 each), pick a target asteroid within 500 WU of the Synodos and a material, and start mining. Each drone hauls 1 SU per cycle, ignoring all risk — just like a station Drone Bay, but mobile. Move the base to reach new belts.
  • Storage — its hold, capped at 200 SU. When full, drone mining pauses until you ferry cargo out. This tab also holds the Move control — relocate the Synodos anywhere for a credits-on-hand fee, exactly like the Custodian.
  • Metochi — the Synodos's built-in tender.

The Metochi tender

Cargo can't be sold from the Synodos directly. Its tender, the Metochi, ferries the haul out. From the Metochi tab, load up to 50 SU from the hold (per-material arrows, like selling), choose an owned shipyard, and dispatch it. The Metochi flies to that yard, deposits the cargo into its storage, and returns to the Synodos — it never docks anywhere itself. At speed 2 it's the fastest ship in the sector.

Like the Custodian, the Synodos is run entirely from the Map. It goes dark while relocating, then resumes mining once it settles at its new position.

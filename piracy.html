Wiki / Mechanics

Piracy

Not every credit comes from a contract. Raid NPC freight fleets and take their cargo by force.

Choose another path. The factions move cargo through the sector whether you work for them or not. With enough fighters, you can take it.

Finding a target

NPC freight fleets travel the sector constantly along real routes between stations. By default they're invisible — you can't raid what you can't see. Install the Cargo Scanner upgrade (see Stations & Upgrades) at a station and any fleet that passes within its range is revealed on the map and stays known for its run.

A revealed fleet shows its faction, an estimate of the cargo it's hauling, and a fleet risk rating from its escorts.

Launching a raid

Tap a revealed fleet on the map and assign one or more docked fighters. Optionally bring a freighter to haul the loot — without one, anything you can't carry is cut loose as a salvage drop at the kill site.

The panel shows your success chance and the maintenance charged for the round trip. Your fighters fly to intercept the moving fleet, and combat resolves on arrival.

Success chance

Win odds scale with your assigned fighters' combined risk aversion against the target's fleet risk. Equal forces are roughly a coin flip; doubling the enemy's strength pushes you toward ~85%.

Outcomes

  • Win — you seize the fleet's commodities. Your freighter hauls the most valuable goods home first (to its cargo cap); the rest drifts as collectable salvage. A close fight may still cost you a fighter or two to attrition.
  • Loss — your raiding party is destroyed and you take on a lien for each ship lost.

On a win the survivors haul the loot back to your nearest owned base and deposit it there.

Reputation cost

Raiding a faction's fleet costs you reputation with that faction (−10 per raid). Piracy and a clean contracting record don't mix — pick your targets with an eye on whose jobs you still want.

Maintenance

Like every dispatch, a raid charges maintenance up front for the round trip, shown before you commit. Ships destroyed in the fight have their unused return-leg maintenance refunded. A failed or empty raid still costs the upkeep to get there and back.

Want to wait for a better intercept? If letting the fleet's path bring it closer would save meaningful travel, the panel offers a delayed launch — see Jobs for how closest-approach timing works.

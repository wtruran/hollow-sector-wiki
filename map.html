Map & Navigation

A living system where every station, asteroid, and ship moves in real time.

Nothing in Hollow Sector stands still. Planets orbit the sun, stations orbit their planets, asteroids drift through belts, and your ships travel between all of them — continuously, in real wall-clock time.

The Map View

The map is the central view of the game. It shows the entire Kepler-11 system at a glance — the sun at the centre, six planets on their orbital paths, asteroid belts, stations, active ships, enemies, and drone runs, all updating live as you watch.

You can pan by clicking and dragging, and zoom with the scroll wheel or pinch gesture. Tap any object on the map to open its detail panel or modal.

Real-Time Orbital Mechanics

All positions in Hollow Sector are calculated from the real wall-clock time at the moment of rendering. There are no "turns" or discrete ticks for movement — every orbit is a continuous trigonometric calculation seeded by the current timestamp.

Planets
Orbit the sun with periods ranging from 1 real day (Kepler-11b, closest) to 7 real days (Kepler-11g, outermost).
Planet-Orbit Stations
Circle their parent planet once every 1 real day, carried along as the planet itself moves around the sun.
Sun-Orbit Stations
Orbit the sun directly on the same path as a planet (co-orbital), or within a belt at their own radius. Periods match their orbital path.
Asteroids
Planet-child asteroids orbit their planet once per 1 real day. Belt asteroids orbit the sun once every 8 real days.
Because everything orbits continuously, the distance between your station and a target asteroid changes throughout the day. A mine run dispatched now may have a different travel time than the same run launched six hours later. The game calculates the actual positions at dispatch time and locks in the route.

Orbital Drift & Planning

Because orbits are continuous, two things are always true:

  • Departure time matters. The game uses a closest-approach search when you try to dispatch a ship, finding the departure window within the next few hours that minimises total travel time. You can accept the optimal window or depart immediately.
  • Drone cycle times vary. Drones recalculate their travel time each cycle based on the current distance to their target asteroid. A drone assigned to a nearby asteroid early in the day may have a longer return trip hours later as the asteroid orbits away.

Distance Units (WU)

All distances are measured in World Units (WU). Ship speed is shown as real minutes per 100 WU — lower is faster. Real travel time = distance (WU) ÷ 100 × speed value. A ship with speed 10 crosses 100 WU in 10 real minutes, 250 WU in 25 real minutes, and so on.

Timers & Refresh Cycles

All in-game timers — ship travel, market refreshes, job board resets — run in real wall-clock time. The sector keeps moving while the app is closed, and the game catches up on everything that happened when you reopen it: a ship that was 10 minutes from landing when you closed the app has landed when you return 10 minutes later. Turn on notifications and your phone tells you the moment a contract completes.

  • Orbital periods are real wall-clock time — a 1-day orbit takes one literal day.
  • Ship travel times are real minutes, as shown in the dispatch panel.
  • Market refreshes every 6 real hours. Job board refreshes every 4 real hours.
Orbital positions persist across sessions. Close the app and reopen it hours later — all planets, stations, and asteroids will be exactly where the math says they should be for that real-world timestamp.

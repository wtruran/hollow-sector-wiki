Jobs

Freight and defense contracts — how they work, what they pay, and how to pick the right ones.

The Job Board

The job board holds up to 20 contracts at a time — roughly split between freight and defense. It refreshes every 4 hours with a completely new pool. Contracts not accepted before the refresh are lost.

You can sort the board by Distance, Credits, SU (freight only), Rating, or Risk using the sort bar. Click a sort option again to reverse direction.

Job Ratings & Tiers

Every contract has a rating from F to A, and the rating drives everything else about the job. A job's tier is chosen first, then its payout, distance, and cargo are all generated to fit that tier. This keeps payouts bounded — a long route can no longer be gamed into a fortune — and makes jobs grow naturally as you do.

Each tier unlocks with faction reputation. A job carries one faction's tag, and you only see it if your reputation with that faction meets the tier's threshold:

Because distance scales with tier, the ship you can afford gates the jobs you can fly. A starter SF1000 (range 500) handles local F runs; reaching C and above needs the range and cargo of an SF3000 or SF4000. The board always shows 20 jobs drawn from the tiers you've unlocked, so a new pilot sees a full slate of flyable F work rather than a thin list.

Freight Contracts

Freight jobs ask you to transport cargo from a pickup station to a dropoff station. The total load is split into individual containers, and the container size scales with the contract's rating (see the table above). A container can't be split across ships — each one must fit whole in a single freighter's hold — but different containers can ride on different ships in the same run.

This means a contract's rating decides what kind of fleet it needs:

  • Low-rated jobs break into small containers (down to 1 SU), so a group of small haulers like the SF1000 can split the load between them.
  • High-rated jobs pack large containers (18 – 25 SU at A tier), each of which needs a big hull such as the SF3000 or SF4000 — a swarm of small ships can't chip away at them.
  • No container ever exceeds 25 SU, the largest freighter hold, so every contract is flyable if you bring the right ships. The assignment panel blocks accepting a job your selected ships can't pack.

Upkeep is deducted from the payout — the game guarantees a minimum profit of ƒ1,500 per job so short local runs never run at a loss.

Risk on Freight

Freight risk is determined at job generation from the total cargo volume. Larger loads — which means higher-tier jobs — attract more pirate attention. The risk level is visible on every job card before you accept.

A contract's tier also sets what it's carrying: higher-rated freight hauls more valuable commodities. The cargo good is shown on every freight job card.

Defense Contracts

Defense jobs hire your fighters to escort a client convoy from one station to another. You assign fighter ships — not freighters. Defense jobs follow the same tier system as freight: payout, distance, and rep gating all come from the tier, and higher tiers carry a higher convoy risk level (F sits around risk 1–2, A around 7–10).

For defense contracts, your assigned fighters count as both the escort and the primary ships — their combined risk aversion determines the fail chance.

Job Phases

Every job moves through two phases visible on the progress bar:

  • Travel to Pickup — ships travel from their current location to the pickup station. The tick mark on the progress bar shows when this ends.
  • In Transit — convoy departs for the dropoff. This is when the attack roll happens (1–3 minutes after departure).
Ships depart from their current location, not your home base. A ship docked at a distant station will have a longer travel-to-pickup phase.

Delayed Departure

Stations, planets, and asteroids all move along their orbits in real time, so a pickup that's far away now may swing much closer soon. When waiting would save a meaningful amount of travel (more than 300 WU), the assignment panel offers a delayed departure — it shows the travel distance and maintenance you'd save and the time to wait.

  • The ship stays docked and fully usable until the scheduled time — it isn't reserved.
  • If the ship isn't available when departure comes (you sent it elsewhere), the contract is cancelled.
  • The same closest-approach option appears for mining, salvage, and raids.
Maintenance is charged up front when a job departs, deducted from the payout (and refunded proportionally if you cancel before the convoy is attacked). The job detail screen shows the exact figure before you commit.

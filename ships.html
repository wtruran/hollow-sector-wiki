Ships

All ship models, their stats, and when to use them.

Speed is shown as real minutes per 100 WU — lower is faster. A speed of 10 means the ship crosses 100 WU in 10 real minutes. Upkeep is the internal rate used to calculate the maintenance fee shown when you dispatch a job — you never pay it directly. Range is the maximum one-way travel distance in WU.

Haulers

Haulers (the SF series) carry cargo containers across the sector. Larger ships carry more SU but move slower and cost more per minute.

SF4000 is the only standard freighter with built-in risk aversion (2). It can partially self-defend without an escort on low-risk runs.
Limited freight ships (SFL series) offer larger cargo or better economics than their standard counterparts. The SFL3000 carries 20 SU — 5 more than the standard SF3000 — at only slightly higher upkeep.

Fighters

Fighters escort hauler and miner convoys. They reduce the attack failure chance by covering the job's risk level with their Risk Aversion stat. Multiple fighters stack their aversion.

Miners

Miners (the MN series) extract raw materials from asteroids. The MN4000 also has risk aversion, making it partially self-defending on return trips.

Mining Speed is a multiplier applied to the base mine time per CU. A lower value means faster extraction. The MN4000 at 0.2× fills its 30 SU hold far faster than the MN1000 at 1.0×.

Limited Ships

One limited ship rotates into the vendor every 2 hours. Limited ships are enhanced variants of standard models with buffed stats (larger cargo for miners, higher risk aversion for fighters, better economics for freighters). They cost more but offer advantages not found on standard hulls.

The limited pool is: SFL2000, SFL3000, FXL2000, FXL3000, MNL2000, MNL3000.

Logistics

Moving ships and cargo around your network — relocation, loading, and manifests.

Your fleet doesn't have to live at your home base. As your network of owned stations grows, you'll want ships staged where the work is — and you'll want a way to get cargo out of places that can't sell it.

Base storage

Every owned base holds a limited amount of cargo. The Storage tab of a base's panel shows how full it is (used / capacity). Sell, refine, produce, or ship cargo out to keep room free — a base that fills up can't take any more until you clear space.

Storage Bay upgrades. Buy a Storage Bay from a base's Upgrades tab to raise its capacity. They stack, so add as many as your operation needs. See Station Upgrades.

When a base is full, nothing is ever silently lost:

  • Ships park and wait. A ship that arrives with cargo it can't fit parks just outside the base, holding its whole load. Tap the ship, make room in storage, and hit Request Docking to unload and dock.
  • Production and buying are blocked. You can't start a production run or buy at the market if the result wouldn't fit — the game tells you up front instead of losing the overflow.
  • Drones pause. Auto-mining drones gather into a small bay buffer and stop when it's full; move the buffer to storage to resume them (see Mining).

Relocating ships

Every docked ship has a Relocate button on its Fleet card. It lists the owned bases within that ship's range — your home base, any ship yards, and a Custodian if you operate one — with the cost to send it there. Pick a destination, confirm, and the ship travels there over real time.

  • Range chaining: a destination beyond the ship's one-hop range is still reachable — the ship hops between stations along the way. The trip takes longer and adds a small docking fee at each stop, so range still matters, but a short-range hull isn't locked out of distant work. A chained route draws its full path on the map, and the ship's detail panel lists the stops it routes through.
  • Cost: the ship's normal maintenance for the one-way trip, plus a docking fee per intermediate stop on a chained route.
  • Cash only: relocation is paid from credits on hand — a lien can't cover it.

Manifests

Freighters carry a visible manifest — the cargo currently aboard. It's shown on the ship's Fleet card and on its map panel, listing both any loaded goods and the goods it's hauling on an active freight contract. A loaded manifest rides along whenever the ship relocates, and is unloaded into the destination's storage on arrival.

Loading cargo for delivery

Any material in a base's storage has a Load button (when a freighter is docked there). Loading is a two-step delivery:

  1. Load — pick a quantity and a docked freighter; the cargo moves from storage onto that ship's manifest (up to its hold capacity).
  2. Relocate — send the loaded freighter to a station or ship yard, where it automatically unloads on arrival.
This is how you get value out of a Custodian: its hold can't be sold from directly. Load its cargo onto a freighter in its barge, then relocate that freighter to a real station to sell.

Transfer risk

Moving valuable cargo attracts pirates. A loaded relocation carries piracy risk scaled to the cargo's value, just like a transfer run — bring an escort for a rich haul. See Risk & Combat for how attack rolls work.

Factions

The four powers of Hollow Sector — reputation, contracts, and unlocks.

The Four Factions

Iron Warden
A military force left behind to maintain order after the corporations withdrew. They enforce sector law and offer high-risk defense contracts to those willing to serve.
Meridian Consortium
Scholars and archivists obsessed with cataloguing the sector's history. They trade in information as readily as cargo, and keep their deepest findings close.
Outer Reach
Rebels and outlaws who found purpose in the forgotten frontier. They run contracts no one else will touch and answer to no authority but their own.
The Aurel Conclave
An alien race that chose to remain when humans arrived, integrating into trade and commerce. Their contracts are lucrative, if you've earned their trust.

Reputation

Each faction has an independent reputation score from 0 to 1,000. Reputation is earned by completing contracts that carry that faction's tag.

Reputation from Bounty Hunting

Destroying enemy ships awards reputation to all factions: 1 rep × the enemy's risk level, capped at 25 rep per kill.

Reputation is per-faction. Maxing one faction's rep unlocks their A-rated contracts but does nothing for another faction's board. Build all four in parallel by accepting contracts from all factions.
B and A ratings are the most profitable jobs in the game. An A-rated freight contract runs at ƒ60,000+/WU — roughly 300× the rate of an F-rated job. Prioritise rep building once your fleet can handle the volume.

Faction Initiatives

Beyond the job board, each faction runs a rolling Initiative — a standing request shown on that faction's detail page, below the reputation bar. Unlike a contract, you never accept or crew an Initiative: it fills passively as you play, and there is no deadline and no way to fail it. Initiatives are a steady second path to reputation, flavored to each faction's character.

When they open

A faction's Initiative program opens once you reach 100 reputation with it — except the faction you joined at the start of the game, whose Initiatives are available immediately. The Eclipse Syndicate runs Initiatives too (raids and smuggling runs), once you've joined it.

What they ask for

Each Initiative asks for one of the following, and progress ticks up from your normal activity:

GoalCounts when you…
Deliverhand over the requested material from your storage, using the Contribute button on the faction's page
Combatdestroy enemy ships (pirate kills for lawful factions; lawful kills for the Syndicate)
Contractscomplete contracts tagged with that faction, from any station
Miningmine ore from any asteroid (crewed runs and auto-drones both count)

Targets scale with your reputation tier, so an Initiative always takes some time to finish.

Rewards & renewal

Completing an Initiative pays a small reputation bump (the point of the feature) plus a modest credit bonus that scales with the size of the ask. The box then shows a brief thank-you, and 1–5 hours later a fresh Initiative takes its place. Each new Initiative is announced by a short, non-critical transmission in that faction's voice, delivered after any other pending messages.

Initiatives run in the background of the whole game. Combat, contract, and mining goals fill on their own as you play; delivery goals just need you to hand over the material on the faction's page. Either way, they're a low-effort way to keep every faction's reputation climbing toward the big unlocks.

Factions

The four powers of Hollow Sector — reputation, contracts, and unlocks.

The Four Factions

Iron Warden
A military force left behind to maintain order after the corporations withdrew. They enforce sector law and offer high-risk defense contracts to those willing to serve.
Meridian Consortium
Scholars and archivists obsessed with cataloguing the sector's history. They trade in information as readily as cargo, and keep their deepest findings close.
Outer Reach
Rebels and outlaws who found purpose in the forgotten frontier. They run contracts no one else will touch and answer to no authority but their own.
The Aurel Conclave
An alien race that chose to remain when humans arrived, integrating into trade and commerce. Their contracts are lucrative, if you've earned their trust.

Reputation

Each faction has an independent reputation score from 0 to 1,000. Reputation is earned by completing contracts that carry that faction's tag.

Reputation from Bounty Hunting

Destroying enemy ships awards reputation to all factions: 1 rep × the enemy's risk level, capped at 25 rep per kill.

Losing Reputation

Reputation isn't only earned. Raiding a faction's freight fleet costs you 10 reputation with that faction per raid. Turn pirate against the people whose contracts you rely on and you'll watch their board dry up.

Reputation Unlocks

Each tier of contract is locked behind a reputation threshold with that contract's faction. Build rep with a faction and its higher-tier jobs begin appearing on the board — see Jobs for the payout and distance bands each tier carries.

At 600 rep, a faction's unique reward ship becomes available to buy: the Iron Warden offers the Custodian (a wandering mobile base), and The Aurel Conclave offers the Synodos (a roving auto-miner with its own cargo tender).
Reputation is per-faction. Maxing one faction's rep unlocks their A-rated contracts but does nothing for another faction's board. Build all four in parallel by accepting contracts from all factions.

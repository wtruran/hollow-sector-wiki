Market

Buying and selling at every station, freight trading, supply depression, and contraband.

The Market Tab

Every station has a market. At a station you own, opening it shows Storage | Market | Upgrades. At a station you don't own, you instead see Station Info | Market, and the market only opens once you reach 200 reputation with the station's controlling faction.

  • Storage — view your inventory, run the Refinery, and load cargo onto docked freighters.
  • Market — buy and sell goods at this station's live prices.
  • Upgrades — purchase and manage station upgrades (Refinery, Drone Bay).

Buying & Selling

The Market tab has a Buy and a Sell button. Pick quantities one unit at a time, up to 10 SU per item per order.

  • At a station you own — trades are instant and use local storage. Buying drops goods straight into the station's storage; selling sells from it.
  • At a station you don't own — you dispatch your own freighters. Buying sends them to collect the goods and haul them to a ship yard you choose. Selling loads goods from one of your yards and runs them to the market, selling on arrival. Send enough freighters to carry the order; they travel as a convoy at the slowest ship's pace.

Cargo unloads into the destination yard's storage on arrival — your freighters never sit on a load.

Market Prices

Prices are randomised per station within a 70%–130% range of each good's base value. A ƒ5,000/SU rare material sells for anywhere between ƒ3,500 and ƒ6,500 depending on the station.

Buying pushes a price up: every 10 SU bought raises that good's price by 10% at that station — the mirror of sell-side supply depression below. Prices refresh every 6 hours, which clears both effects. If the timer reaches zero while the screen is open, prices update automatically.

Supply Depression

Each time you sell a material at a station, its effective price drops. Every 10 SU sold reduces the price by 10% at that station. At 100 SU sold, the price reaches zero.

Don't flood one station. Sell large hauls across multiple stations or wait for the daily sales volume reset at midnight. The reset clears all supply depression, restoring full prices.

Trading Between Stations

Because you can buy at one market and sell at another, price gaps are profit. Buy where a good is cheap or plentiful and ship it to a station paying more, or to one you haven't flooded with sales. Owning a ship yard near a lucrative market makes you the destination for buy orders and the source for sell orders.

Freight orders carry risk in transit, based on the value of the load — higher value convoys attract more pirate attention.

Contraband & Smuggling

Four illicit goods trade outside the legal market:

  • Bought only at The Market, the Eclipse Syndicate's black-market station.
  • Sold at any station for a premium.
  • The price never falls — it only climbs as you buy, and resets at the next market refresh.

Every station has a Security level. Selling contraband there risks the crew being caught: the chance is Security × 10%, rolled once for the whole order. If you're caught, the contraband is seized, you pay legal fees charged as a lien, and you lose reputation with the station's faction. Cargo manifests are sealed, so the size or value of a load doesn't change the odds.

The Shroud is the only ship that beats Security. A smuggling order slips through untouched only if every freighter carrying it is a Shroud.
